History of Indian Capital Markets
1875
BSE Founded — Asia's First

The Bombay Stock Exchange established under a banyan tree on Dalal Street — the oldest stock exchange in Asia and one of the first in the world.

1956
Securities Contracts Act

The Indian government formalised regulation of securities trading, setting the legal foundation for modern capital markets.

1988–1992
SEBI & NSE Born

SEBI was established as the regulatory authority in 1988 (statutory powers in 1992). The National Stock Exchange launched in 1992, introducing electronic order-matching and transforming market transparency.

2000
Derivatives Era Begins

NSE launched index futures and options on Nifty 50, ushering in a new era of risk management. India's derivatives market now ranks among the world's largest by contract volume.

2008
Global Crisis — 60% Drawdown

The Sensex fell from 21,000 to under 8,000 during the global financial crisis — one of the steepest corrections in Indian market history, followed by a V-shaped recovery.

2020
Covid Crash & Retail Revolution

Markets collapsed 40% in 30 days in March 2020, then rallied 100%+ within a year. Demat account openings surged from 40M to 130M+ as retail investors flooded the market.

2024–2026
All-Time Highs & Quantitative Age

Sensex crossed 80,000 and Nifty 24,000. India became the world's 4th-largest equity market. Algorithmic trading accounts for over 50% of NSE volume, with ML models driving institutional strategies.
